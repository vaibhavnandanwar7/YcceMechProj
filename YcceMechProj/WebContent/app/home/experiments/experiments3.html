Sine Bar :

A sine bar consists of a hardened, precision ground body with two precision ground cylinders fixed at the ends. The distance between the centers of the cylinders is precisely controlled, and the top of the bar is parallel to a line through the centers of the two rollers. The dimension between the two rollers is chosen to be a whole number (for ease of later calculations) and forms the hypotenuse of a triangle when in use.

When a sine bar is placed on a level surface the top edge will be parallel to that surface. If one roller is raised by a known distance, usually using gauge blocks, then the top edge of the bar will be tilted by the same amount forming an angle that may be calculated by the application of the sine rule.

The hypotenuse is a constant dimension - (100 mm or 10 inches in the examples shown).

The height is obtained from the dimension between the bottom of one roller and the table's surface.

The angle is calculated by using the sine rule. Some engineering and metalworking reference books contain tables showing the dimension required to obtain an angle from 0-90 degrees, incremented by 1 minute intervals.

Sin(angle) = Perpendicular/Hypotenuse

Angles may be measured or set with this tool.


Apparatus : Surface plate, Sine bar, Slip gauges, V-Block, Work piece, Dial indicator.

SINE BAR
The sine bar is the most accurate instrument for measuring angles. It consists of a accurate straight rectangular bar made of high quality steel and having two accurately lapped cylindrical plugs or rollers at the ends.
SLIP GAUGE
Slip Gauges are known as Gauge Blocks. They are Precise Measuring instruments. These Slip Gaugesare universally accepted end Standard of length. You know, Johanson first introduced the slip gauges, so they can be called as Johanson gauges as well.
SURFACE PLATE
A surface plate is a solid, flat plate used as the main horizontal reference plane for precision inspection, marking out (layout), and tooling setup.
V-BLOCK
A V-block is a square or rectangular precision-made block used to securely hold a cylindrical workpiece on a milling machine or drill press. They are called V-blocks because they have at least one centrally located v-shaped groove upon which parts are positioned.
DIAL INDICATOR
Dial indicators are instruments used for making and checking linear measurements.

Applications :
  1. Sine-bar is used to set or locate the workpiece at a given angle.
  2. To check the measuring of unknown angles in the workpiece.
  3. To Check the unknown angles on the heavier components.
  4. Some specially designed Sine bars are used to mount the workpiece to do the conical shape machining for the workpiece.
